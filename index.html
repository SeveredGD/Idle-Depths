IDLE DEPTHS
A guide to the descent
The Basics

Idle Depths is an idle ARPG. Your hero fights automatically — enemies spawn, you attack, they die, loot drops. Your job is to manage skills, gear, and progression to push deeper into harder content.

You Will Die — That's Okay

Death is a core part of the loop, not a failure state. When a boss is killing you, don't keep throwing yourself at it — use 🌾 Farm Zone to idle a cleared zone instead. Farm for gear and XP until you're strong enough to push further. The game rewards patience. If a boss feels impossible, you're meant to farm first.

Class Philosophies

Sorceress — A glass cannon built around elemental damage and crowd control. She hits hard and can freeze, burn, shock, and chill entire packs, and has an innate affinity for elemental status effects — her procs land more often than other classes. Defensively she relies on her Energy Shield passive and Warmth regen rather than raw HP. Play her as a high-output nuker: invest in AoE skills for farming and single-target spells for boss fights. Her damage ceiling is the highest of any class, but she punishes greediness.

Warrior — A bruiser who thrives on momentum. He builds Rage by attacking and taking hits, then spends it through his active Technique for powerful effects. More HP and a base block chance make him naturally durable, but his damage output depends heavily on how well you manage Rage. The Warrior rewards staying in the fight and planning around his Technique — Execute for burst, Last Stand for survival, Item Find for farming.

Rogue — A specialist who commits to a playstyle from the start. The Rogue has no skills by default — first choose a Specialization in the T3 tab (Assassin, Toxicologist, or Mage Hunter), which unlocks your three T1 combat skills and defines how you fight. Then pick an active Scheme from the T2 tab (Lucky, Pilfer, or Excavation) for a passive bonus that enhances your loot and gold. The Assassin opens every fight with a free guaranteed crit Backstab then switches to Blade Dance or Flurry — high burst, bleed-oriented. The Toxicologist stacks poison and neurotoxins, debilitating enemies while DoTs wear them down with HP sustain on kills. The Mage Hunter cycles through elemental attacks, building and consuming spell shields, with the strongest status effect bonuses of any class. All Rogue T1 skills scale more dramatically than other classes since there are no T2 combat spells — invest heavily in your chosen spec passive to amplify everything.

Combat

Your character attacks automatically based on the skill selected in the right panel. Each zone has 10 mobs then a boss. Killing the boss opens a shop and advances to the next act. There are 5 acts per difficulty. Clearing Act 5 on a difficulty unlocks the next one. If you die on any difficulty you restart the act you died on — you never lose more than one act of progress.

Status Effects

Each damage type has a chance to apply a status effect on hit. 🔥 Burn (fire) deals damage over time in ticks. ⚡ Shock (lightning) fires an instant bonus hit when it lands. ❄ Chill (cold) stacks a slow on the enemy's attack speed — keep hitting to build stacks and refresh the timer before it fades. 🩸 Bleed (physical) stacks independently, each stack running its own damage-over-time timer. The Sorceress has a natural talent for elemental statuses and procs them more reliably than other classes.

Skills

You gain a skill point every level. Spend them in the Skill Tree (right panel). Each class has its own Tier 1 and Tier 2 skills — Tier 2 unlocks at mid-levels with enough Tier 1 investment. Passives (or Techniques for the Warrior) live in the third tab. The Rogue works differently — the T2 tab holds Schemes (pick one active bonus), and the T3 tab holds Specializations (pick one to unlock your T1 combat skills). You can respec all points — you start with a free respec per difficulty unlocked, then it costs gold (doubling each time). Use the Skill Loadout panel below the skill tree to assign a farm skill and a boss skill — the game swaps automatically when a boss spawns and back when mobs resume.

Gear & Loot

Items drop with four rarities: Normal, Magic, Rare, and Unique. Better items are automatically equipped if they score higher than what you have. The Vault holds the top items per slot — click a vault item to swap it in. Lock a slot to prevent auto-replacing it. Uniques that don't fit are queued for review — a purple button appears in the left panel when items are pending.

💡 A little Magic Find goes a long way. Even modest MF meaningfully shifts your drop quality. Don't overlook it when evaluating gear — a piece with solid MF can outperform a pure damage item over a long farming session.

The Shop

After every boss kill a shop opens briefly (pauseable). You can buy items on offer, an MF Blessing that temporarily boosts your Magic Find, a random Gem Fragment for reforging, or gamble a random item. The shop auto-closes and advances to the next act — hit Pause if you need more time. Use the 💊 Top Off button (once per act, costs a little gold) to heal to full before a tough boss.

Reforging

Gem Fragments drop rarely from mobs and more reliably from bosses. Each gem type favors different stats — Fortune (MF/Gold), Ruin (Dmg/XP), Tempest (Speed/Dmg), Ember (Elemental). Select a gem in the Reforging tab, then click the ↻ button on any Magic or Rare vault item to reforge its stats. Reforging costs gold and one gem. Stats can roll differently every time — sometimes better, sometimes worse. The Reforging tab blinks when you have gems to spend.

⚠ Every reforge carries a chance of destroying the item — and that chance grows with each successive reforge on the same piece. Hover over an item's reforge button to see the current risk before committing.

Loops & Farming

The 🎯 Target Boss button repeatedly farms a specific boss for a prepaid gold cost. The 🌾 Farm Zone button farms mobs without a boss at a lower cost with increased rare pack chance. Inside Farm Zone you can choose a Hunt Pack type for extra gold — Ghost Packs drop jewelry and trinkets only, Undead Packs drop weapons and armor, Goblin Packs focus on gold with the occasional jewelry find. Rare packs are groups of bonus mobs marked ★ that drop extra loot.

Build Priority

In the left panel under Build Priority, you can flag up to a few stats as important. The scoring system will weight those stats heavily when deciding whether to auto-equip or vault an item, making sure gear that matches your build gets prioritised. Click the ▾ button next to the Hero panel title to collapse secondary stats and save screen space.

Relics

Relics are special items that charge from the deaths around you — mob kills grant one charge, boss kills refill all charges. When enough charges are stored, the relic activates and releases its power. The relic frame sits below the combat log; click Use to activate manually, or choose an automatic trigger mode: When Full (fires the moment charges max out), On Boss (fires when a boss spawns), or On Expiry (refires automatically when the previous use ends).
